How CoinQuest works

Two halves of one wallet. Earn on the left, spend on the right.

Half one · the game

Walk it into existence

  1. Put on your glasses. Quest 3, Quest Pro, or Ray-Ban Meta. Open the app and your surroundings light up with coins anchored at GPS points.
  2. Walk your usual routes. Commute, dog walk, coffee run — anywhere pedestrian-friendly becomes a collection lane.
  3. Look + tap to collect. Combo chains multiply rewards. Daily challenges drop themed coin trails across the neighborhood.
  4. Coins land in your wallet. Tied to your identity, syncable across devices, never lost.
Half two · the marketplace

Spend it with neighbors

  1. List anything you'd trade. Goods (used bikes, baked goods, art), services (lessons, rides, haircuts), or time (an hour helping with a move).
  2. Price in coins. The seller sets the rate. The community votes on fairness with their wallets.
  3. Browse, claim, meet up. Buyers find listings on the in-glasses map or the web app. Coins are held in escrow until both sides confirm.
  4. Sellers spend their earnings. Same wallet, opposite direction. The loop closes.

Common questions

Is this gambling?

No. CoinQuest is a skill- and activity-based rewards game. You don't pay to play and you don't wager coins. Coins are earned through walking and challenges, not chance-based purchases.

Can I buy coins with money?

No. Coins can only be earned by walking, completing challenges, or selling something in the marketplace. There is no cash-in path. This is what keeps the loop closed and the economy honest.

What can I list in the marketplace?

Anything legal that you'd be comfortable handing to a neighbor. Used goods, baked goods, lessons, services, freelance work, your time. Restricted categories — alcohol, weapons, prescription medication, regulated services — are blocked at posting.

What does it cost?

The app itself is free. Optional cosmetics can be purchased, but every cash and gift-card payout is funded by us, not by other users. Marketplace trades are coin-only — we take a small coin fee on completed trades to fund moderation.

Will I owe taxes on payouts and trades?

Possibly. In the U.S., the IRS treats marketplace trades as barter income at fair market value, and cash redemptions over $600 in a year trigger a 1099. We issue 1099-B forms for barter activity above the threshold and 1099-MISC for cash redemptions. Your accountant is the right person to ask about your specific situation.

What about my privacy and location data?

Location is processed on-device whenever possible. We store the minimum needed to validate coin collection and prevent spoofing. You can request a full export or deletion anytime.

Is it safe to walk while wearing AR glasses?

We only spawn coins along sidewalks and pedestrian zones, never in roadways. The app freezes coins at unsafe crossings and shows obstacle and traffic alerts in your view. Always look where you're going — coins will wait.

What ages can play?

CoinQuest is open to users 13 and up. Players under 18 need a guardian to approve cash redemptions, and we don't process payouts to minors directly.

The legal stuff, in plain English

We take this seriously. Here's the short version of what governs a cashless rewards community.

Sweepstakes & gaming law

CoinQuest is structured as a no-purchase-necessary skill and activity rewards program. Coins are earned by walking, not by paying or wagering, so it sits outside U.S. gambling definitions in every state we've reviewed.

Barter taxation (IRS)

The IRS treats every marketplace trade as taxable barter income at the fair market value of what you received. We register as a barter exchange under IRC §6045(c)(3) and issue 1099-B forms to members whose trade volume crosses the threshold.

Money transmission

Coins are a closed-loop unit of account, not currency. They cannot be converted to cash between members, only earned in the game and spent in the marketplace. Cash redemptions to the user's own bank or PayPal go through licensed partners who hold the MTL.

Marketplace facilitator & sales tax

In states with marketplace facilitator laws, we collect and remit sales tax on taxable trades. Casual sales of used personal items are generally exempt; professional services and new goods are not. Our checkout flow prompts sellers to flag the difference.

Restricted categories

You can't list alcohol, tobacco, firearms, prescription medication, live animals, ride-share services, short-term lodging, childcare, or anything else that requires a professional license in your state. Listings are screened at posting and at checkout.

Liability & consumer protection

CoinQuest is a venue for member-to-member trades, not a party to them. Sellers are responsible for what they list; buyers for what they accept. Escrow, dispute resolution, and a member-led arbitration panel handle disagreements before anyone reaches for a lawyer.

Privacy & location

We follow GDPR and CCPA standards globally. Location data used for coin validation is processed on-device when possible, encrypted in transit, and never sold. Marketplace listings show approximate distance, not addresses.

Minors & COPPA

No users under 13. Members 13–17 can play the game but cannot list services, accept in-person trades, or redeem to cash without verified parental approval.

AR safety & meeting safety

Coins never spawn in roadways. The overlay pauses during unsafe movement. For marketplace meetups, we surface recommended public exchange spots (often local police station lobbies or designated safe-trade zones) and an optional escort feature.
